# Time to apply the brakes to runaway AI, says pioneer

## Why it matters
Geoffrey Hinton's remarks at the UN Digital World Conference frame AI as 'a very fast car with no steering wheel' requiring regulatory intervention. His call for governance alongside the UN's policy discussions provides important context on the international push for AI regulation. UNCTAD projects the global AI market growing from $189B (2023) to $4.8T (2033)—an economy larger than Japan's—underscoring why governance matters.

## Lede (original)
If AI is 'a very fast car with no steering wheel' then regulation must provide one, insists Nobel laureate and Artificial Intelligence pioneer Geoffrey Hinton, speaking at the Digital World Conference co-organized by the UN Research Institute for Social Development.
