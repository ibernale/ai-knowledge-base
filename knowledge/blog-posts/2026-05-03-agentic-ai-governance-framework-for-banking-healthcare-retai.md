# Agentic AI Governance Framework for Banking, Healthcare, Retail, and Supply Chain

## Why it matters
Yale's Chief Executive Leadership Institute conducted a cross-industry review of agentic AI deployments and emerging governance practices, prompted by Claude Mythos's paradigm shift in AI capabilities. The framework addresses accountability, transparency, bias, and data privacy across banking, healthcare, retail, and supply chain deployments. Unlike abstract policy discussions, this provides industry-specific guidance on what to assess before deployment, what to govern during it, and case studies of companies already navigating these challenges. For enterprise practitioners, it's a practical governance playbook as agentic AI moves from proof-of-concept to production.

## Lede (original)
While leaders in the artificial intelligence industry dubbed 2025 the year of Agentic AI, 2026 marks the shift from capability to execution. Unlike large language models, AI agents can interact with external tools, execute multiple steps to complete a task, learn from their results, and iterate.
