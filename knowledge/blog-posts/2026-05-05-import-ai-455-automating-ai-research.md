# Import AI 455: Automating AI Research

## Why it matters
Jack Clark's weekly newsletter delivers a landmark essay arguing that fully automated AI R&D may arrive by 2028. He synthesizes publicly available evidence across benchmarks, model evaluations, and frontier lab announcements—including Anthropic's model-training speedup progression (2.9× → 52×) and OpenAI's stated goal of an 'automated AI research intern by September 2026'. The piece frames the transition as already underway, citing both research automation benchmarks and the explicit capital allocation of $500M+ startups aiming at recursive self-improvement. Essential reading for anyone tracking the acceleration timeline.

## Lede (original)
I'm writing this post because when I look at all the publicly available information I reluctantly come to the view that there's a likely chance (60%+) that no-human-involved AI R&D - an AI system powerful enough that it could plausibly autonomously build its own successor - happens by the end of 2028.
