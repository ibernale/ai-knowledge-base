# OpenAI launches the OpenAI Deployment Company to help businesses build around intelligence

## Why it matters
OpenAI has launched a major new $4 billion enterprise services company with 19 global partners including TPG, Bain Capital, and McKinsey. The move signals that the AI race is shifting from model building to enterprise deployment and integration. The acquisition of Tomoro brings 150 'Forward Deployed Engineers' who will embed directly into client organizations. This is OpenAI's most significant structural move since launching ChatGPT, directly competing with Anthropic's parallel announcement of a similar enterprise services venture. Working practitioners should note this signals model vendors are now competing on implementation depth, not just model capability.

## Lede (original)
OpenAI is launching the OpenAI Deployment Company, a new company designed to help organizations build and deploy AI systems they can rely on every day across their most important work. The OpenAI Deployment Company will extend OpenAI's ability to embed engineers specialized in frontier AI deployment, known as Forward Deployed Engineers, or FDEs, into organizations working on complex problems in demanding environments.
