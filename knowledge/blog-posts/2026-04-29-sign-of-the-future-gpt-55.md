# Sign of the future: GPT-5.5

## Why it matters
Ethan Mollick provides an accessible first-look analysis of GPT-5.5 from someone with early access. His side-by-side comparisons of complex coding prompts (procedural 3D simulation) across o3, Kimi K2.6, and GPT-5.5 Pro offer practitioners concrete benchmarks beyond official evals. He notes that while technical progress continues impressively (math, counting are now trivial), the 'jagged frontier' persists—GPT-5.5 still struggles with long-form fiction, showing repetitive metaphors and dialogue patterns. A grounded reality check on where frontier models actually stand.

## Lede (original)
I had early access to GPT-5.5, and I think it is a big deal. It is a big deal because it indicates that we are not done with the rapid improvement in AI. It is also a big deal because it is just plain good. And it is a big deal because even with all of this, the frontier of AI ability remains jagged.
