# What comes next with open models

## Why it matters
Nathan Lambert predicts three model classes will emerge: true frontier (closed), open frontier attempting parity, and small specialized models. He argues closed models will take 'leaps and bounds' in 2026 in directions open models can't follow, forcing a reconceptualization of open model value propositions. His framework of commoditizing complements vs. trillion-dollar costs challenges assumptions about Meta's Llama strategy.

## Lede (original)
2025 was the year where a lot of companies started to take open models seriously as a path to influence in the extremely valuable AI ecosystem. Through 2026, closed models are going to take leaps and bounds in performance in directions that it is unlikely for open models to follow.
