# Import AI 455: Automating AI Research

## Why it matters
Jack Clark's deep analysis argues we're approaching automated AI R&D: Anthropic's models achieved 52x speedup on LLM training optimization tasks (from 2.9x with Opus 4 to 52x with Mythos Preview), and top agents score 25-28% on an automated research benchmark where humans score 51%. The essay traces the evidence across coding benchmarks, Anthropic's system cards, and OpenAI's explicit goal of building 'an automated AI research intern by September 2026.' Clark forecasts a proof-of-concept of 'a model end-to-end trains its successor' within a year or two, with implications for recursive self-improvement that he plans to work through for the rest of 2026.

## Lede (original)
AI systems are about to start building themselves. The purpose of this essay is to enumerate why I think the takeoff towards fully automated AI R&D is happening. I'll discuss some of the consequences of this, but mostly I expect to spend the majority of this essay discussing the evidence for this belief.
