# AI co-clinician: researching the path toward AI-augmented care

## Why it matters
Google DeepMind announces its AI co-clinician research initiative, a significant step toward triadic care where AI agents interact with patients under physician supervision. The system demonstrated zero critical errors in 97 of 98 primary care queries using the NOHARM framework, outperforming existing AI tools in blind physician evaluations. On RxQA medication knowledge benchmarks, AI co-clinician surpassed frontier AI systems especially on open-ended questions. This represents DeepMind's evolution from diagnostic AI (MedPaLM, AMIE) to collaborative clinical deployment models—a template for safety-critical agentic systems.

## Lede (original)
Health systems worldwide are striving for better outcomes, lower costs, and an improved experience for both patients and clinicians. However, progress is constrained by a global shortage of clinical experts, with the World Health Organization predicting a shortfall of more than 10 million health workers by 2030. While AI is often seen as the key to bridging this gap, it has not yet been able to fully meet the needs of clinicians and patients. That's why, today, we are announcing our AI co-clinician research initiative, to explore how AI could better amplify doctors' expertise and deliver higher quality care to patients.
