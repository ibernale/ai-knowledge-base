# Vibe coding and agentic engineering are getting closer than I'd like

## Why it matters
Simon Willison reflects on how the distinction between 'vibe coding' (casual AI-assisted programming) and 'agentic engineering' (rigorous AI-augmented development) is blurring in his own practice. Key insight: when you can produce 2,000 lines of code per day instead of 200, the entire software development lifecycle designed around human coding speed breaks down. He argues usage history matters more than code quality signals—a vibe-coded project used daily for two weeks is more valuable than a pristine but untested one. This captures a fundamental shift in how practitioners should evaluate AI-generated codebases.
