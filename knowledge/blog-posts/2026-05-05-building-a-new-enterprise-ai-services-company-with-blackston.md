# Building a new enterprise AI services company with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs

## Why it matters
Anthropic announced a $1.5B joint venture with Blackstone, Goldman Sachs, Hellman & Friedman, and other PE giants to deploy Claude directly into mid-market enterprise operations. The structure embeds Anthropic engineers inside portfolio companies—a model that directly challenges traditional consulting firms like McKinsey and Accenture. This signals a major shift: AI companies are moving beyond API licensing toward full-stack implementation services, with built-in distribution through PE-owned company networks. Watch for similar moves from OpenAI (reportedly pursuing a deal with TPG and Bain).

## Lede (original)
Anthropic, Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs announced the formation of a new AI services company. The organization will work with mid-sized companies across sectors to bring Claude into their most important operations.
