# The distillation panic

## Why it matters
Nathan Lambert argues that 'distillation attacks' is a dangerous misnomer that will irrevocably associate all distillation with adversarial behavior. While Chinese labs hacking APIs to extract signal is a real concern, distillation generally is a core technique needed to diffuse AI capabilities through academic and economic activities. He poses an interesting counter: if Chinese companies are addicted to distillation, they'll never learn techniques needed to take an outright lead—cutting off this crutch may give short-term US advantage but push competitors toward more competitive trajectories. Essential reading for post-training researchers.

## Lede (original)
'Distillation attacks' is a horrible term for what is happening right now. Yes, some Chinese labs are hacking or jailbreaking APIs to attempt to extract more signal from model APIs — stopping this is important to maintain the U.S.'s lead in AI capabilities. Referring to this as distillation attack is going to irrevocably associate all distillation with this behavior.
