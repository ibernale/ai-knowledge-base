# The next phase of the Microsoft-OpenAI partnership

## Why it matters
Microsoft announced fundamental changes to its OpenAI partnership: Microsoft's license is now non-exclusive, they no longer pay revenue share to OpenAI (revenue share from OpenAI to Microsoft continues through 2030 with a cap), and OpenAI can now serve products across any cloud provider. Microsoft retains license to OpenAI IP through 2032 and remains primary cloud partner with first-ship rights unless they cannot support necessary capabilities. This restructuring affects every enterprise AI strategy.

## Lede (original)
Microsoft remains OpenAI's primary cloud partner, and OpenAI products will ship first on Azure, unless Microsoft cannot and chooses not to support the necessary capabilities. OpenAI can now serve all its products to customers across any cloud provider. Microsoft will continue to have a license to OpenAI IP for models and products through 2032.
