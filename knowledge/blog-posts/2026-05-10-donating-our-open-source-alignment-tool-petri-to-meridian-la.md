# Donating our open-source alignment tool Petri to Meridian Labs

## Why it matters
Anthropic has transferred its Petri alignment evaluation tool to Meridian Labs, an independent nonprofit, marking a significant shift toward treating AI safety evaluation as shared infrastructure rather than proprietary methodology. The Petri 3.0 release includes architectural changes that separate auditor and target models for greater adaptability, a new 'Dish' add-on that runs tests using models' actual production system prompts to counter eval-awareness, and integration with Anthropic's Bloom tool. The UK AI Security Institute already uses Petri for evaluating model sabotage propensity, making this handoff crucial for maintaining credibility of safety evaluations across the industry.

## Lede (original)
We're now updating Petri to its third version. Here are some of the biggest changes: Adaptability—Petri 3.0 involves major architectural changes that allow users to adapt it to more uses, in particular by splitting the auditor model and the target model into separate components that can be tweaked separately. We're also giving Petri a new home. We have handed over its development to Meridian Labs, an AI evaluation nonprofit.
