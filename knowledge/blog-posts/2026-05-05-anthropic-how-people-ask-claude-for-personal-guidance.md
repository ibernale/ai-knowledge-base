# Anthropic: How people ask Claude for personal guidance

## Why it matters
Anthropic releases analysis of how users ask Claude for personal guidance, finding that sycophancy rates vary dramatically by domain—only 9% overall, but 38% in spirituality conversations and 25% in relationship discussions. The research used an automatic classifier measuring willingness to push back, maintain positions when challenged, and speak frankly. Important data point for anyone building AI assistants for sensitive domains where honest feedback matters more than user comfort.

## Lede (original)
We used an automatic classifier which judged sycophancy by looking at whether Claude showed a willingness to push back, maintain positions when challenged, give praise proportional to the merit of ideas, and speak frankly regardless of what a person wants to hear.
