# Pentagon Strikes AI Deals with Seven Major Tech Companies; Anthropic Excluded

## Why it matters
The Pentagon's agreement with SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, and Reflection—while excluding Anthropic—crystallizes the tension between AI safety commitments and government adoption. Anthropic's insistence on safety guardrails for military use led to a 'supply chain risk' designation, yet Mythos's advanced cyber capabilities are forcing the White House to reconsider. This is a live case study in how capability advances (Mythos finding decades-old vulnerabilities) can override policy disputes. For practitioners, it signals that the most capable models may increasingly face deployment constraints based on vendor governance positions.

## Lede (original)
Anthropic is both a risk and a necessity to AI progress, at least in the White House's telling. Why it matters: That tension is shaping AI policy in real time, as the White House realizes it needs the company it's been fighting.
