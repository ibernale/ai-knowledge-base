# The Latent Space: Foundation, Evolution, Mechanism, Ability, and Outlook

## Why it matters
Comprehensive survey treating latent space as a computational paradigm rather than just a representation layer. Organizes the fragmented literature through dual lenses of Mechanism (Architecture, Representation, Computation, Optimization) and Ability (Reasoning, Planning, Modeling, Perception, Memory, Collaboration, Embodiment). Provides unified framework for understanding how latent reasoning, world models, and implicit computation relate to each other—essential for researchers working on next-generation architectures.

## Abstract (original)
Latent space is rapidly emerging as a native substrate for language-based models. While modern systems are still commonly understood through explicit token-level generation, an increasing body of work shows that many critical internal processes are more naturally carried out in continuous latent space than in human-readable verbal traces.
