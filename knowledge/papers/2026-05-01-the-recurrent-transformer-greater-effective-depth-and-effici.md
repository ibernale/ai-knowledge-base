# The Recurrent Transformer: Greater Effective Depth and Efficient Decoding

## Why it matters
Harvard researchers introduce layerwise recurrence within standard Transformer layers, enabling greater effective temporal depth and enhanced representational capacity. The model achieves lower cross-entropy loss on language modeling benchmarks with fewer layers while maintaining efficient training via a novel tiling algorithm and reducing KV cache size for faster inference. Addresses the compute-memory tradeoff that limits current Transformer scaling.

## Abstract (original)
Researchers at Harvard University developed the Recurrent Transformer, an architecture that introduces layerwise recurrence within standard Transformer layers, enabling greater effective temporal depth and enhanced representational capacity. This model achieves lower cross-entropy loss on language modeling benchmarks with fewer layers while maintaining efficient training via a novel tiling algorithm and reducing key-value (KV) cache size for faster inference.
