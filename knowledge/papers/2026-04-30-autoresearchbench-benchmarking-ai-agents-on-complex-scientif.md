# AutoResearchBench: Benchmarking AI Agents on Complex Scientific Literature Discovery

## Why it matters
AutoResearchBench exposes a severe capability gap: even frontier LLMs that dominate general web browsing benchmarks achieve only ~9% accuracy on autonomous scientific literature discovery tasks. The benchmark contains 1,000 problems across 8 CS areas and reveals that shallow heuristic matching fails for deep research requiring multi-step verification over full-text papers. This is critical for anyone building AI research assistants or scientific agents, as it shows the gap between BrowseComp-style web navigation and genuine autonomous research capability.

## Abstract (original)
AutoResearchBench is a benchmark for autonomous scientific literature discovery that evaluates AI agents' ability to conduct deep and wide research tasks with high difficulty, achieving low accuracy rates even among powerful LLMs. The benchmark contains 1,000 problems spanning eight core areas of computer science, built through a full-text-first human–machine pipeline and verified by human experts.
