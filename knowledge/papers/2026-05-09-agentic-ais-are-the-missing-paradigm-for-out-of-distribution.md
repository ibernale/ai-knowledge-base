# Agentic AIs Are the Missing Paradigm for Out-of-Distribution Generalization in Foundation Models

## Why it matters
This paper argues that agentic AI architectures—where models can take actions, observe outcomes, and iterate—represent a fundamentally different paradigm for achieving out-of-distribution generalization compared to scaling alone. The authors demonstrate that agentic loops enable foundation models to handle distribution shifts that cause pure inference-time scaling to fail. This theoretical framing is useful for practitioners deciding when to invest in agentic scaffolding vs. simply using larger models.
