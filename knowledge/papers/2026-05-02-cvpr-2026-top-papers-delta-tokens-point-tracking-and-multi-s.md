# CVPR 2026 Top Papers: Delta Tokens, Point Tracking, and Multi-Scale Attention

## Why it matters
CVPR 2026 accepted 4,090 papers from 16,092 submissions. Highlighted top papers include: 'A Frame is Worth One Token' introducing delta tokens for efficient generative world modeling; 'Real-World Point Tracking with Verifier-Guided Pseudo-Labeling'; and 'MSPT: Efficient Large-Scale Physical Modeling via Parallelized Multi-Scale Attention'. These represent the current frontier in computer vision research, with implications for robotics, video understanding, and multimodal systems. The delta token work is particularly relevant for efficient video generation architectures.

## Abstract (original)
Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition is a massive conference. In 2026 alone, 16,092 papers were submitted, and 4,090 were accepted. This repository curates the crème de la crème of CVPR publications including work on depth estimation, generative models, object tracking, physical modeling, pose estimation, and segmentation.
