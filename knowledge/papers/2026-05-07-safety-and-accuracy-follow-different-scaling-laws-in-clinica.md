# Safety and accuracy follow different scaling laws in clinical large language models

## Why it matters
This paper demonstrates that safety and accuracy in clinical LLMs follow different scaling laws—a finding with significant implications for medical AI deployment. As models scale, accuracy improvements may not correlate with safety improvements, meaning healthcare organizations cannot assume larger models are automatically safer. The work provides empirical grounding for the safety-capability tradeoff discussion and informs how clinical AI systems should be evaluated and deployed.

## Abstract (original)
We demonstrate that safety and accuracy follow different scaling laws in clinical large language models. This finding has implications for how medical AI systems should be evaluated and deployed, as scaling model size may improve accuracy without corresponding improvements in safety.
