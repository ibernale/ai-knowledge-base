# Agentic World Modeling: Foundations, Capabilities, Laws, and Beyond

## Why it matters
This survey provides a unified framework for world models across fragmented research communities (RL, vision, robotics). It operationally defines world model value by the quality of decisions they enable for downstream agents, introducing JEPA and DINOv2 as state encoders alongside 'Thinking with Blueprints' for object-centric reasoning. The framework addresses conceptual fragmentation where papers report progress under incompatible definitions, offering practitioners a common language to evaluate world models regardless of domain-specific implementation.

## Abstract (original)
The ambition to build internal models of reality has a long intellectual history. The phrase world model is now widely used across research communities, but its precise technical meaning varies considerably. In reinforcement learning, agents learn transition structure to imagine futures before acting. In computer vision, world models often denote video or 3D generators that maintain visual dynamics and temporal coherence.
