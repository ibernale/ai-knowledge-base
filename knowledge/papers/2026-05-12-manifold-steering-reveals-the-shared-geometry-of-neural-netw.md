# Manifold Steering Reveals the Shared Geometry of Neural Network Representation and Behavior

## Why it matters
This Stanford-led paper challenges the Linear Representation Hypothesis that has dominated interpretability work. The authors demonstrate that neural network representations follow curved manifold geometries, not flat Euclidean spaces, and that respecting this geometry is essential for effective model steering. They show 'manifold steering' achieves 2.8x improvement in naturalness across language and vision tasks. For practitioners building steering/intervention systems, this recasts the core problem from 'finding the right direction' to 'finding the right geometry.' The paper has implications for mechanistic interpretability, alignment interventions, and control methods.

## Abstract (original)
Neural representations carry rich geometric structure; but does that structure causally shape behavior? To address this question, we intervene along paths through activation space defined by different geometries, and measure the behavioral trajectories they induce. We find that steering along the activation manifold, which we term manifold steering, yields behavioral trajectories that follow the behavior manifold, while linear steering produces unnatural intermediate states.
