# Rethinking Reasoning-Intensive Retrieval: Evaluating and Advancing Retrievers in Agentic Search Systems

## Why it matters
Accepted at ACL 2026, this paper evaluates retrieval systems in agentic search contexts where reasoning is required to find and synthesize information. The work addresses a gap between traditional RAG evaluations (optimized for simple keyword matching) and the more complex retrieval patterns needed by autonomous agents. Provides both evaluation methodology and improved retriever designs for agentic systems.

## Abstract (original)
We rethink reasoning-intensive retrieval and evaluate how well existing retrievers perform in agentic search systems. We propose advances to retriever design specifically optimized for the reasoning-heavy retrieval patterns that autonomous agents require.
