# Position: agentic AI orchestration should be Bayes-consistent

## Why it matters
This position paper argues that while making LLMs themselves Bayesian belief-updating engines remains computationally intensive, the control layer orchestrating agentic AI systems should apply Bayesian decision theory. The paper provides concrete design patterns for calibrated beliefs and utility-aware policies in human-AI collaboration, tool-calling decisions, and resource allocation. Relevant for practitioners building multi-agent orchestration systems who want principled decision-making under uncertainty.

## Abstract (original)
LLMs excel at predictive tasks and complex reasoning tasks, but many high-value deployments rely on decisions under uncertainty, for example, which tool to call, which expert to consult, or how many resources to invest. While the usefulness and feasibility of Bayesian approaches remain unclear for LLM inference, this position paper argues that the control layer of an agentic AI system (that orchestrates LLMs and tools) is a clear case where Bayesian principles should shine.
