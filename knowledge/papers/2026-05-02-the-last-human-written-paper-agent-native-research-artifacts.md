# The Last Human-Written Paper: Agent-Native Research Artifacts

## Why it matters
This paper introduces the Agent-Native Research Artifact (ARA), a protocol that replaces narrative papers with machine-executable research packages. ARA addresses the 'Storytelling Tax' (lost failed experiments) and 'Engineering Tax' (missing implementation details) that compilation into papers imposes. The protocol has four layers: scientific logic, executable code, exploration graph preserving failures, and evidence grounding. On PaperBench, ARA raises QA accuracy from 72.4% to 93.7% and reproduction success from 57.4% to 64.4%. This proposes a fundamental shift in how research is published for agent consumption.

## Abstract (original)
Scientific publication compresses a branching, iterative research process into a linear narrative, discarding the majority of what was discovered along the way. This compilation imposes two structural costs: a Storytelling Tax and an Engineering Tax. We introduce the Agent-Native Research Artifact (Ara), a protocol that replaces the narrative paper with a machine-executable research package structured around four layers.
