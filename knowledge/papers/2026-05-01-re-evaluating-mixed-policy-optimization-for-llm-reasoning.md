# Re-evaluating Mixed-Policy Optimization for LLM Reasoning

## Why it matters
Critical methodological paper that identifies and corrects bugs in widely-used SFT training frameworks for LLM reasoning. Demonstrates that a correctly implemented SFT-then-RL pipeline consistently outperforms current mixed-policy approaches on mathematical reasoning benchmarks, often with greater computational efficiency. Forces the field to re-examine recent claims about mixed-policy optimization—some celebrated results may have been artifacts of implementation bugs rather than genuine algorithmic advances.

## Abstract (original)
This research re-evaluates mixed-policy optimization methods for large language model (LLM) reasoning by identifying and correcting bugs in widely used supervised fine-tuning (SFT) training frameworks. It demonstrates that a correctly implemented SFT-then-reinforcement learning (RL) pipeline consistently outperforms current mixed-policy approaches on mathematical reasoning benchmarks, often with greater computational efficiency.
