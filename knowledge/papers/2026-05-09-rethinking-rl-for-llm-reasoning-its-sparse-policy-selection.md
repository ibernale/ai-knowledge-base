# Rethinking RL for LLM Reasoning: It's Sparse Policy Selection, Not Capability Learning

## Why it matters
This paper challenges the prevailing narrative that RL fine-tuning teaches LLMs new reasoning capabilities. Instead, the authors argue RL primarily performs sparse policy selection—surfacing reasoning patterns already present in the base model rather than learning new ones. This has significant implications for post-training strategy: if RL is selection rather than capability learning, practitioners should focus on base model quality and diverse pre-training rather than expecting RL to compensate for missing capabilities.
